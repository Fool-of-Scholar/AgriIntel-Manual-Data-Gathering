Visual Inspection 👀

Color Logic (1-10): Scores 8-10 mean optimal ripeness and vibrant color, required for Grade A. Scores 6-7 are acceptable for Grade B. Below 6 indicates under-ripe or severe discoloration.

Size Logic (1-10): Scores 8-10 mean uniform, standard market size (Grade A). Scores 6-7 are slightly irregular but acceptable (Grade B). Extreme sizes (too small/large) drop to Grade C.

Defects Logic: '0' means flawless (Grade A). '1-2 minor' allows slight surface blemishes (Grade B). '3+ visible' or 'Severe' (mold, deep cuts) immediately drops to Grade C.

Texture Logic: 'Smooth & Firm' is required for Grade A. 'Rough/Bumpy' can be Grade B. 'Shriveled/Soft' indicates water loss or rotting, automatically dropping to Grade C.

Nutrients 🧪 Optional

Nitrogen (N): Essential for chlorophyll. Low levels lead to stunted growth and pale yellow leaves.

Phosphorus (P): Drives root development and flowering. Deficiency causes dark green or purple leaf edges.

Potassium (K): Regulates water and fruit quality. Low K results in weak stems and "burnt" leaf margins.

Grading Standards 📋

⭐ Grade A (Premium)

Logic: Perfect color and size. 0 Defects. Smooth Texture.

Best for export and high-end markets. Maximum shelf life.

👍 Grade B (Standard)

Logic: Good color and size. Max 2 minor defects. Non-shriveled.

Good for local retail. Minor surface marks allowed.

🏭 Grade C (Processing)

Logic: Poor color/size OR 3+ Defects OR Shriveled Texture.

Best for jams, juices, or immediate processing.
